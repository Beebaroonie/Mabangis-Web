JUNBERT, JUEGO TODO FMA USA AMBASSADOR

The Philosophy of Mabangis

My journey began in 2022. While four years is a relatively short timeline, my progress was driven by a relentless training schedule—often 3 to 4 times a week for 6 hours a session—and a specific, analytical way of seeing movement. This development wasn't a matter of luck; it was a systematic breakdown of mechanics. This approach is the heart of my personal style, Mabangis. It is a philosophy born from necessity and resourcefulness. I have frequently been in situations where I lacked both goals and resources, yet I found ways to move forward—much like building this website entirely on my own. I believe that even when you lack external tools, the solution is already in your head if you look deep enough.

The Silent Teachers and Lineage

Before I ever sought out formal instruction, the weapons themselves were my teachers. I collected specific types of weaponry that demanded I adapt my body mechanics and move differently for each one. This self-taught exploration of structural alignment and physics laid the groundwork for my official training.

My formal refinement and lineage eventually came under the guidance of Guro Robert Grossman in the art of Balintawak. I have since integrated experience from Boxing, Muay Thai, and Sanda into my practice. For me, FMA is about the practical application of these systems, and my journey of refinement remains a lifelong process.

Community and Cultural Representation

Beyond the training, I am currently dedicated to building community by aiding three separate businesses, driven by the work itself rather than financial compensation. My career and my focus remain with Juego Todo.

I have performed at events such as Asian Pacific Heritage Month and Filipino American Month to showcase the sophistication of Philippine blade systems. These are among the most advanced weapon-based arts in the world, and I believe Filipinos and Filipino-Americans deserve a system they can be truly proud of. My goal as an ambassador is to use Juego Todo as a bridge to bring people together and to help others understand movement with the same clarity that I do.
